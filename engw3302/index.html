About Me

I am a third-year student majoring in electrical engineering and minoring in physics.

My documents all pertain to the topic of unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) in agriculture. I address the topic from three different audiences over three papers: once to general audiences unaware of the topic, once to an audience with partial knowledge of the topic, and once to an audience with complete knowledge of the topic.

Each paper is written with different genres, purposes, and motives in accordance with the audience they are targetting. These documents have greatly improved my writing style and have allowed me to better understand how to write in the technical professions.

Initial Inquiry

Analysis of Benefits and Drawbacks of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles in Precision Agriculture

This document is addressed to audiences completely unaware of the topic at hand.

Its purpose is to inform the reader about the core debate behind UAVs in precision agriculture.

Researched Document

A Proposal Regarding a Design Approach for the Sale of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles in U.S. Precision Agriculture

This document is addressed to the Product Strategy & Future Airplane Development division at Boeing.

Its purpose is to convince this division, which specializes in commercial airfcraft, to consider a different approach to the problem of creating drones for agriculture.

Public Document

UAVs in Agriculture: The Most Significant Current Issue in the Drone Industry

This document is addressed to general technology enthusiasts, specifically towards drone specialits.

Its purpose is to relate topics in precision agriculture to legal discourse and to possible solutions. It is more specific than the initial inquiry, but more broadly applied than the researched document.
